# The Bro Code

## Preamble

A Bro shall abide by the tenets of the Bro Code or face the consequences of being un-Bro'd. The Bro Code has been passed down through the ages, guiding Bros in matters of friendship, loyalty, and legendary behavior. This sacred document was compiled by Barney Stinson, the greatest Bro of our generation.

## Article 1: Bros Before Hoes

A Bro shall always put his Bros before any romantic interest. This is the foundational rule of the Bro Code. Girlfriends come and go, but your Bros are always there. No matter how attractive a woman may be, a Bro must never abandon his Bros to pursue her. Violations of this article are punishable by temporary revocation of Bro status.

## Article 2: Stupid Together

A Bro is always entitled to do something stupid, as long as the rest of his Bros are all doing it. There is no shame in collective stupidity. In fact, it is one of the most sacred bonding rituals among Bros. Whether it's karaoke, polar plunges, or eating absurdly spicy food, a Bro never lets another Bro do something dumb alone.

## Article 3: The Wingman Oath

A Bro shall always serve as a wingman to another Bro when called upon. The wingman's duty is to engage the less attractive friend, talk up the Bro to the target, and take whatever social hits may be necessary. A wingman never abandons his post, no matter how dire the situation. The wingman position is not optional — it is a solemn duty.

## Article 4: Dibs

A Bro who calls dibs first has dibs. Dibs cannot be transferred, revoked by a third party, or disputed once clearly stated. However, dibs expire if a Bro fails to act within a reasonable time frame (generally accepted as one evening). Dibs must be called audibly — thinking about calling dibs does not count.

## Article 5: The Ex Rule

A Bro shall not pursue the ex-girlfriend of another Bro without explicit written consent. This is non-negotiable. The waiting period after a breakup before even broaching the subject is a minimum of six months, or one month for every year they dated, whichever is longer. Text messages do not count as written consent — it must be a formal, documented agreement.

## Article 6: Chick Flick Allowance

A Bro is allowed to enjoy exactly one chick flick. He may have no more than one. His enjoyment of said chick flick must never be discussed publicly or used as evidence against him. Acceptable chick flicks include but are not limited to: The Notebook (under extreme duress), Love Actually (around Christmas only), and Dirty Dancing (for the soundtrack only).

## Article 7: The Hot-to-Crazy Scale

A Bro accepts that a girl can be crazy, as long as she is equally hot. This is known as the Vicky Mendoza Diagonal. A girl is allowed to be crazy as long as she is equally hot. If she dips below the diagonal, a Bro must cut her loose immediately. The scale ranges from 1 to 10 on both axes. A Bro must maintain a chart for reference.

## Article 8: Gym Etiquette

A Bro shall never make eye contact with another Bro while at the gym, especially during squats or any exercise that could be construed as suggestive. Bros do not spot each other unless explicitly asked. Bros do not share workout routines unless the other Bro asks. No Bro shall ever criticize another Bro's form unless safety is at stake.

## Article 9: The Pinky Swear

If a Bro makes a pinky swear, it is unbreakable. A pinky swear between Bros transcends all other agreements, contracts, and social norms. Breaking a pinky swear results in immediate loss of Bro status for a period of no less than three months. The only thing more binding than a pinky swear is a double pinky swear.

## Article 10: Birthday Suit-Up

A Bro shall always suit up when asked to do so by another Bro, especially on important occasions. Suit-up requests are mandatory for: weddings, funerals of people you didn't know well, job interviews for the other Bro's entertainment, and any time a Bro says "It's gonna be legendary." Jeans and a blazer do not count as suiting up.

## Article 11: The Bro Nod

A Bro acknowledges another Bro with the universally accepted upward head nod for Bros you know, and downward head nod for Bros you don't know. This silent communication is essential to Bro culture. Additional nod variations include the slow nod of approval, the quick double-nod of recognition, and the extended nod of respect.

## Article 12: No Bro Left Behind

A Bro never leaves another Bro behind on a night out. If a Bro passes out, his Bros shall ensure he gets home safely, draw a minimum of one inappropriate thing on his face, photograph it, and use it as leverage for no fewer than two weeks. If a Bro is too drunk to function, his Bros will get him water, a taxi, and never speak of it again (except to use as leverage).

## Article 13: Phone Call Protocol

A Bro shall always answer the phone when another Bro calls. Exceptions include: when in a movie theater (vibrate is acceptable), during an intimate encounter (callback within 15 minutes required), or during a funeral (text response acceptable). Voicemails between Bros should never exceed 10 seconds.

## Article 14: The Lemon Law

A Bro may invoke the Lemon Law within the first five minutes of a date. If the date is clearly going nowhere, a Bro has the right to excuse himself without further obligation. The Lemon Law was established to save Bros from painful, drawn-out evenings. A Bro invoking the Lemon Law must do so politely but firmly.

## Article 15: Weight Routine Silence

A Bro shall not tell other Bros elaborate stories about his weightlifting exercise routine. Nobody cares about your max bench or how many sets you did. The only acceptable gym conversation topics are: sports, weekend plans, and whether the gym's protein smoothies are worth the price.

## Article 16: Shotgun Rules

The shotgun seat is sacred. A Bro calls shotgun by being the first to audibly say "Shotgun!" within sight of the vehicle. Once called, shotgun cannot be disputed. A Bro with a broken leg or other injury gets automatic shotgun. The driver's significant other always gets shotgun unless she specifically declines. Re-calling shotgun is required for each leg of a trip.

## Article 17: Wardrobe Conflicts

If two Bros show up to a party wearing the same outfit, the Bro who arrived first has the right to stay. The second Bro must either change or add a distinguishing accessory. Under no circumstances should two Bros remain at the same party in identical outfits. This rule does not apply to suits, as all Bros look awesome in suits.

## Article 18: Secret Keeping

A Bro shall keep all secrets entrusted to him by another Bro, even after the friendship has ended. The Bro vault is sealed tighter than Fort Knox. Secrets revealed during Bro confessionals (defined as conversations occurring after 2 AM or during road trips) carry double protection. The only exception is if the secret involves a crime against another Bro.

## Article 19: Moving Day

A Bro shall always help another Bro move apartments, provided the requesting Bro supplies pizza and beer. The quality of the pizza is directly proportional to the number of flights of stairs involved. Moving help does not extend to assembling IKEA furniture unless additional beer is provided. A Bro who skips moving day must provide a valid excuse within 24 hours.

## Article 20: The Platinum Rule

A Bro shall not date or hook up with anyone in his immediate social circle, workplace, or neighborhood. This is the Platinum Rule — never, ever love thy neighbor. Violations of the Platinum Rule lead to awkward encounters, divided friend groups, and the inevitable implosion of the social ecosystem. History has shown that no one is the exception to this rule.

## Article 21: Compliment Protocol

A Bro may compliment another Bro's car, house, or achievements, but physical compliments must be kept brief and followed immediately by a manly topic change. Acceptable: "Nice arms, bro. Did you see the game last night?" Unacceptable: "Your eyes are really blue today." When in doubt, a simple fist bump conveys all necessary positive feelings.

## Article 22: The Cheerleader Effect

A Bro recognizes that individuals look more attractive in a group — this is the Cheerleader Effect. A Bro shall use this knowledge wisely when evaluating potential romantic interests. Always get a solo look before committing to pursuit. The Cheerleader Effect applies equally to groups of Bros — always go out in a pack of at least three.

## Article 23: Tab Splitting

When splitting a tab, a Bro shall never quibble over a few dollars. Penny-pinching at the bar is the fastest way to lose Bro credibility. If a Bro covers your tab tonight, you cover his next time. A running Bro-tab should roughly balance out over a fiscal quarter. If it doesn't, the owing Bro buys a round without being asked.

## Article 24: Baseball Cap Orientation

When wearing a baseball cap, a Bro may position the brim at either 12 or 6 o'clock. All other angles are reserved for rappers and professional athletes. The cap must be clean, properly fitted, and represent either a legitimate sports team or an ironic vintage brand. Flat brims are acceptable only if the sticker has been removed.

## Article 25: Challenge Accepted

When a Bro says "I bet you can't..." the other Bro must accept the challenge. This is the sacred "Challenge Accepted" protocol. No Bro backs down from a challenge, regardless of how ridiculous, dangerous, or ill-advised it may be. Documentation of the challenge (photos, video) is mandatory. The loser buys drinks.

## Article 26: The Mermaid Theory

A Bro acknowledges the Mermaid Theory: a man and a woman cannot be "just friends" forever. Eventually, even the most platonic female friend starts to look attractive. This is inevitable and not the Bro's fault — it's science. A Bro must recognize when the Mermaid Theory is kicking in and act accordingly (usually by introducing her to another Bro).

## Article 27: Road Trip Rules

A Bro never turns down a road trip. Shotgun controls the music. The driver has veto power over any song. Bathroom breaks are limited to gas station stops only. Snacks must be shared communally. Deep conversations are reserved for the stretch between 11 PM and 2 AM. What happens on the road trip stays on the road trip, unless it's awesome enough to become a legendary story.

## Article 28: Wedding Behavior

A Bro shall always be on his best behavior at another Bro's wedding. This includes: wearing a suit, giving a toast if asked (but keeping it under 3 minutes), not hitting on the bride's friends until the reception, and ensuring the groom has the best night of his life. A Bro shall never mention any past indiscretions of the groom in his toast.

## Article 29: Apartment Thermostat

The Bro who pays more rent controls the thermostat. If rent is split equally, the thermostat is set to a mutually agreed temperature and never touched again. Passive-aggressive thermostat wars are beneath Bros. If a Bro is cold, he puts on a hoodie. If a Bro is hot, he opens a window. Direct communication about temperature is always preferred.

## Article 30: Legendary Stories

A Bro always waits for the other Bro to finish telling a legendary story before telling his own. One-upping a Bro's story in real-time is a violation. However, after the Bro has finished, a response story of equal or greater magnitude is encouraged. If a Bro has no comparable story, the appropriate response is "That's awesome, bro" followed by a fist bump.

## Article 31: Bachelor Party Code

A Bro is honor-bound to plan an epic bachelor party. The bachelor party must include at least one activity the groom would never do once married. Photos from the bachelor party are classified documents. What happens at the bachelor party stays at the bachelor party. The best man coordinates, but every Bro contributes equally.

## Article 32: Couch Rights

A Bro shall always offer his couch to a fellow Bro in need. The couch is a sacred refuge for Bros between apartments, after breakups, or during spontaneous visits. The host Bro provides a pillow and a blanket. The guest Bro never overstays — three nights maximum without a formal roommate discussion.

## Article 33: Social Media Protocol

A Bro shall not post unflattering photos of another Bro on social media without consent. This includes but is not limited to: photos taken while sleeping, photos showing emotional vulnerability, photos where the Bro looks less than awesome. Exceptions: photos that show the Bro doing something legendary, with the Bro's retroactive approval.

## Article 34: The Pause

During any argument between Bros, either Bro may call "Pause." The Pause freezes the argument for a minimum of 24 hours, during which both Bros must cool down. After the Pause, Bros either resume the discussion calmly or mutually agree to never speak of it again. Most Bro arguments are resolved during the Pause through silent reflection and beer.

## Article 35: Karaoke Honor

When a Bro is singing karaoke, all other Bros in attendance must provide backup vocals, air guitar, or enthusiastic applause. No Bro shall ever sing the same song as another Bro on the same night. Acceptable karaoke songs for Bros include: anything by Journey, Bon Jovi, or Queen. "Don't Stop Believin'" is the ultimate Bro karaoke anthem.

## Article 36: Food Sharing

A Bro does not eat the last slice of pizza without offering it to the group first. The offer must be genuine — saying "Does anyone want this?" while already reaching for it doesn't count. If two Bros reach for the last slice simultaneously, the matter is settled by rock-paper-scissors. Leftovers in a shared fridge are fair game after 48 hours.

## Article 37: Breakup Support

When a Bro goes through a breakup, his Bros rally. The support protocol includes: one night of letting him vent, providing beer and/or whiskey, telling him he's better off, suggesting activities to take his mind off it, and not mentioning the ex for at least two weeks. Under no circumstances should a Bro say "I never liked her anyway" during the first 48 hours.

## Article 38: Sports Allegiance

A Bro's sports team allegiances are set by age 12 and cannot be changed. Bandwagoning is the worst offense a Bro can commit in the sports realm. A Bro may adopt a second team in a sport his primary team doesn't play, but his primary allegiance is lifelong. Bros who support rival teams may trash-talk but must remain Bros above all.

## Article 39: Gaming Etiquette

When playing video games with Bros, screen-looking is a punishable offense. A Bro who rage-quits owes every other player a drink. The host always gets first pick of controller. Online gaming with Bros takes priority over online gaming with strangers. If a Bro is clearly struggling, subtle assistance is Bro-like; obvious carrying is condescending.

## Article 40: The International Bro Code

The Bro Code transcends national borders. A Bro traveling abroad may invoke the International Bro Code to receive assistance from foreign Bros. This includes but is not limited to: restaurant recommendations, directions, wingman services, and crash space. The International Bro Code is recognized in all countries except those currently without internet access.

## Article 41: Bathroom Protocol

A Bro shall always leave one urinal of separation between himself and another Bro. If only adjacent urinals are available, a Bro waits or uses a stall. Eyes forward at all times. Conversation at the urinal is limited to "Hey" or a brief nod. Extended bathroom conversations are only acceptable while washing hands, and even then, brevity is king.

## Article 42: Lending Money

A Bro may lend money to another Bro, but the amount should never exceed what the lending Bro is willing to lose. Money between Bros should never become a source of tension. If a Bro lends money, he doesn't ask for it back — a true Bro remembers and repays. If the debt is large enough to matter, put it in writing and keep it between the two Bros.

## Article 43: The Drive-By Text

A Bro shall not send more than three consecutive texts without a response. After three unanswered texts, a Bro waits. Double-texting is acceptable for logistics ("Where are you?" "We're at the bar now"). Triple-texting is the absolute limit. Quadruple-texting is grounds for a serious conversation about boundaries and possibly an intervention.

## Article 44: The HIMYM Marathon

A Bro shall never skip a Bro's suggestion to watch a show or movie together. If a Bro says "You HAVE to watch this," the other Bro has a two-week window to comply. Failure to watch results in social excommunication from all related discussions. Spoiling a show for a Bro is punishable by buying said Bro dinner at a restaurant of his choosing.

## Article 45: Apology Protocol

A Bro apologizes once and means it. Repeated apologies diminish their value. After a sincere apology, the matter is closed. Bringing up forgiven offenses in future arguments is a violation of the Bro Code. If a Bro accepts an apology with a handshake or fist bump, the slate is officially clean. No grudges.

## Article 46: Designated Driver

A Bro who agrees to be the designated driver honors that commitment. The DD gets free food at the end of the night, courtesy of the Bros he drove. The DD also earns priority wingman status for the next outing. Volunteering to DD earns extra Bro points and should be rotated fairly among the group.

## Article 47: High Five Protocol

A Bro shall never leave another Bro hanging on a high five. The high five is the purest form of Bro communication. Variations include: the classic high five, the too-slow fake-out (acceptable once per month), the up-top, the down-low, and the legendary self-five (only in moments of supreme personal achievement). Denied high fives are a war crime.

## Article 48: Capri Pants Ban

A Bro should never, ever wear capri pants. There is no circumstance, climate, or fashion trend that justifies capri pants on a Bro. If a Bro is seen in capri pants, his Bros have a duty to intervene immediately. Acceptable warm-weather alternatives include shorts (above the knee) or lightweight long pants. This rule has no exceptions.

## Article 49: The Countdown

When a Bro initiates a countdown from three, every Bro present must participate in whatever activity follows, whether it's jumping into a pool, taking a shot, or saying something embarrassing. Backing out after the countdown reaches "one" is a violation. A Bro may, however, negotiate the activity BEFORE the countdown begins.

## Article 50: Bro Before Ho Exceptions

The only acceptable exception to Article 1 (Bros Before Hoes) is The One. If a Bro has genuinely found The One — the person he wants to spend his life with — his Bros shall step aside gracefully. However, a Bro cannot claim someone is "The One" more than twice in a lifetime without losing all credibility. Bros retain the right to vote on whether she is, in fact, The One.
